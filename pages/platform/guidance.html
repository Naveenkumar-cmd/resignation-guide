5 Things Every Employee Must Do Before Resigning

A resignation is a pivot point in your career story. Make sure you're turning at the right moment.

01

Build a financial runway of 6–12 months

Ensure you have enough savings to sustain your lifestyle for 6 months without income. This removes desperation from your job search and lets you choose wisely. Include EMIs, rent, SIPs, and insurance in your calculation.

Action: Calculate your monthly burn rate. Target 6× that amount in liquid savings before resigning.
02

Update your skills & resume 3 months before applying

The market rewards people who are actively learning. Before you resign, complete at least one certification, update your LinkedIn with recent projects, and secure 2–3 recommendations from current colleagues.

Action: Identify the 2 skills most in-demand for your target role. Begin one course this week.
03

Have at least 2–3 active opportunities before quitting

Never quit a job to search for a new one. Start applying while employed — it signals stability and gives you negotiating leverage. Interviews while employed are more productive and less stressful.

Action: Set up job alerts on Naukri, LinkedIn, and Instahyre. Apply to 5 target roles this week.
04

Have the honest conversation with your manager first

Many resignations are preventable. A direct conversation about growth, salary, or project allocation often leads to solutions. You may get a promotion, transfer, or flexible arrangement without leaving.

Action: Book a 1:1 with your manager this week. Prepare 3 specific, structured concerns.
05

Check your legal and contractual obligations

Review your employment contract for notice period (typically 30–90 days in India), non-compete clauses, and bonding conditions. Factor in stock vesting — don't leave money on the table.

Action: Pull out your offer letter today. Note your notice period and all exit obligations.

Alternatives to Resigning You Haven't Considered

Resignation is the nuclear option. Often, there are smarter moves that achieve your goals without the risk.

🔄

Internal Transfer

Move to a different team, project, or BU within your organisation. You keep seniority, benefits, and institutional knowledge while getting a fresh start.

Best for: escaping a toxic manager or seeking new challenges
📚

Educational Leave / Sabbatical

Many companies offer 3–12 month leaves for education or personal reasons. Take the break you need and return to a secure role — without burning bridges.

Best for: burnout, MBA aspirants, career pivots
🤝

Negotiate Role Redesign

Propose a redesigned role: new responsibilities, different reporting structure, or remote work. Many managers prefer this over losing a good employee.

Best for: feeling underutilised or misaligned with current work
⏰

Reduced Hours / Part-Time

Negotiate a temporary reduction in hours to manage burnout or pursue a side venture. A 3–6 month bridge that gives you breathing room without full commitment.

Best for: entrepreneurs, new parents, health management
💬

Counter-Offer Negotiation

Use an outside offer to negotiate a raise, promotion, or title change internally. Companies often pay to retain good talent — but only if you ask strategically.

Best for: those primarily motivated by compensation or title
🌍

Global / Remote Opportunity

Explore global remote roles within your company or in the international market. Working remotely for a US/EU company from India can offer 2–4× domestic compensation.

Best for: senior professionals in tech, consulting, finance

How to Know It's Actually the Right Time to Resign

Timing your exit perfectly can mean the difference between a smooth transition and a stressful scramble.

✅ Strong Signs You're Ready

  • You have 6+ months of liquid savings
  • You have at least one strong job offer
  • Your skills are actively in-demand
  • Your professional network is strong
  • You've exhausted all internal alternatives
  • Mental health is declining despite genuine effort
  • You have a clear vision of what comes next

⚠️ Warning Signs — Wait Longer

  • Acting on emotion after a bad week
  • Less than 3 months of savings
  • No concrete offers or active pipeline
  • Vesting cliff is less than 6 months away
  • Haven't had the manager conversation yet
  • Job market in your domain is currently weak
  • Unsure what you actually want next

⏳ Best Times of Year to Switch

  • Jan–Mar: Peak hiring — budgets fresh, most opportunities
  • Sep–Oct: Second best — Q3 hiring surge
  • Post-appraisal: Leverage your rating for negotiation
  • Avoid: Nov–Dec (budget freezes, slow hiring)
  • Avoid: Immediately after joining bonus vesting

Your First 90 Days After Resignation

What you do immediately after leaving shapes the entire arc of your next chapter.

Week 1–2

Decompress & Debrief

Give yourself 1–2 weeks to mentally detach. Reflect on what you want — not just what you're running from. Write a "career clarity" document: what energises you, what doesn't, and non-negotiables for your next role.

Week 3–4

Activate Your Network

Reach out to 20 people with personal, specific messages — not a mass broadcast. Let former mentors, ex-colleagues, and industry friends know you're exploring. 70% of roles are filled through referrals.

Month 2

Build Your Presence & Apply

Update LinkedIn with a compelling headline and recent achievements. Optimise your resume for ATS. Apply to target companies — 10 well-researched applications beat 50 generic ones every time.

Month 3

Interview, Evaluate & Negotiate

Practice structured interview responses using the STAR method. Research each company's culture and leadership. When an offer arrives, always negotiate — most employers have 10–20% flexibility built in.
