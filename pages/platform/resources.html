📄 Resignation Letter Templates

Professional templates for every scenario. One click to copy.

Standard

Professional Resignation

[Date] Dear [Manager's Name], I am writing to formally notify you of my resignation from my position as [Job Title] at [Company Name], effective [Last Working Day]. This decision has not been easy. I am deeply grateful for the growth opportunities and experiences [Company Name] has provided. I have learned tremendously from my colleagues and the work we have accomplished together. I am committed to a smooth transition and will do my best to complete pending tasks and hand over my responsibilities properly before my last day. Thank you for your understanding and support. Warm regards, [Your Name]
Short Notice

Short Notice Period

[Date] Dear [Manager's Name], I am writing to inform you of my resignation from [Company Name] effective [Date], due to [personal circumstances / urgent opportunity]. I understand this is shorter than the required notice period and sincerely apologise for any inconvenience. I am happy to assist in any way possible — including preparing detailed documentation of my responsibilities to ensure a smooth handover. I am grateful for the experience and growth I gained at [Company Name] and hope to maintain a positive professional relationship going forward. Sincerely, [Your Name]
Career Change

Career Pivot Resignation

[Date] Dear [Manager's Name], Please accept this letter as formal notice of my resignation from [Job Title] at [Company Name], effective [Last Day]. After significant reflection, I have decided to pursue a career transition into [new field/domain]. This is a long-considered decision aligned with my long-term personal and professional goals. Working at [Company Name] has been a privilege. The skills, relationships, and experiences I have gained here will continue to shape my career. I am fully committed to supporting the team through this transition and will ensure proper handover of all responsibilities. Thank you for your understanding, [Your Name]

One short stint is rarely damaging if the rest of your career shows stability. Be prepared with a clear, honest reason. A pattern of multiple short tenures raises red flags for recruiters — avoid this repeatedly.

Counter-offers are tricky. Studies show 80% of employees who accept counter-offers still leave within 12 months — because the underlying reasons haven't changed, only the salary. Always evaluate the root cause of your desire to leave first.
