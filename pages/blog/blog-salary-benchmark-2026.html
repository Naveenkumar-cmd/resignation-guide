2026 Salary Benchmark Report:
What Are Professionals Really Earning?

We analysed 3,200+ assessments to pull the most accurate salary data across functions and experience bands in India.

Methodology

This report is based on anonymised data from 3,247 Resignation Readiness Assessments completed on our platform between January 2025 and January 2026. Salary figures are self-reported and represent total annual compensation (base + fixed bonus) in Indian Rupees. Stock options and variable pay are excluded unless otherwise noted.

All figures represent the median compensation at each band, with the 25th and 75th percentile range provided to show realistic spread. Data is segmented by function, total experience, and broad location tier (Tier 1 metros vs others).

These figures reflect real professionals, not job postings. Job postings systematically overstate market compensation at the high end; this report represents what people are actually earning.

Technology (Engineering & Product)

Technology professionals continue to command the widest compensation range of any function, driven by significant variance between product companies, IT services firms, and funded startups.

  • 0–2 years: ₹5–9 LPA (median ₹6.8L)
  • 3–5 years: ₹12–22 LPA (median ₹16.4L)
  • 6–10 years: ₹22–45 LPA (median ₹31.2L)
  • 10+ years (senior/lead): ₹40–90 LPA (median ₹58L)

Product Managers earn a 15–25% premium over equivalent-experience engineers at the 6–10 year band. Tier 1 metro professionals (Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Pune, Delhi-NCR) earn 18–30% more than counterparts in Tier 2 cities in the same function.

Finance & Accounting

Finance compensation is more compressed than technology, with the exception of investment banking, private equity, and senior CFO roles which command significant premiums.

  • 0–2 years: ₹4–7 LPA (median ₹5.2L)
  • 3–5 years: ₹8–15 LPA (median ₹11L)
  • 6–10 years: ₹15–28 LPA (median ₹20L)
  • 10+ years (VP/Director): ₹28–60 LPA (median ₹38L)

Investment banking professionals at 3–5 years experience earn 40–60% more than the general finance median. CA-qualified professionals command a consistent 20–30% premium over non-CA peers at equivalent experience levels.

Marketing & Sales

Sales compensation is the most variable of all functions due to the variable pay component. The figures below represent fixed + guaranteed variable only.

  • 0–2 years: ₹4–7 LPA (median ₹5.5L)
  • 3–5 years: ₹9–16 LPA (median ₹12L)
  • 6–10 years: ₹16–32 LPA (median ₹22L)
  • 10+ years (Head/VP): ₹30–65 LPA (median ₹42L)

High-performing enterprise sales professionals with consistent track records at 6–10 years routinely earn 50–80% above the fixed compensation median once variable is included. Digital marketing specialists earn 15–25% more than traditional marketing generalists at equivalent experience.

Operations & HR

Operations and HR are the most compressed functions in terms of compensation growth trajectory, though senior leadership roles in both have seen meaningful increases in recent years.

  • 0–2 years: ₹3.5–6 LPA (median ₹4.8L)
  • 3–5 years: ₹7–13 LPA (median ₹9.5L)
  • 6–10 years: ₹13–24 LPA (median ₹17L)
  • 10+ years (Director/CHRO/COO): ₹25–55 LPA (median ₹33L)

HR Business Partners in technology companies earn 20–35% more than peers in traditional industries at equivalent experience, reflecting the tight talent market in tech.

Key Insights From the Data

  • The 5–7 year inflection point: Compensation growth accelerates significantly between years 5 and 7 for professionals who have made at least one strategic job move. Those who stay with one employer through this period consistently earn less than those who have moved at least once.
  • Stagnation premium: Professionals who haven't changed roles in 4+ years are earning, on average, 22–35% below market rate for their function and experience. Loyalty is not being financially rewarded in the current market.
  • The gender gap persists: Female respondents report earning 12–18% less than male counterparts in equivalent roles and experience bands across all functions. This gap is narrowest in technology and widest in sales.

Using This Data in Negotiations

Knowing your market value is the single most important preparation you can do before any compensation conversation — whether negotiating a counter-offer, discussing a promotion, or evaluating a new offer. Use these benchmarks as a starting point, and supplement with role-specific data from platforms like LinkedIn Salary, Glassdoor, and direct conversations with peers.

If you suspect you're being significantly underpaid, our assessment will confirm whether your compensation is a signal to move — or one piece of a larger picture worth examining more carefully.
